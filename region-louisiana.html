Wetland Loss & Land Subsidence

Louisiana loses a football field of coastal wetland every 100 minutes. The Mississippi River Delta has lost over 5,000 km² since the 1930s — driven by levees, oil canals, and accelerating sea level rise. At 9+ mm/yr, Louisiana experiences the fastest relative sea level rise in the United States. Communities that existed for generations now sit underwater or are cut off from the mainland.
